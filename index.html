Projects

  • Project 1

    NDVI (Normalized Difference Vegetation Index) is a remote sensing tool used to measure the health and productivity of vegetation by analyzing light reflectance in the red and near-infrared spectrum. It provides a simple, efficient, and cost-effective way to monitor soil changes, specifically vegetation cover, and identify areas that require attention. NDVI is widely used in agriculture, forestry and environmental monitoring.This page will find information and a project on NDVI changes in Greece from 2016-2020.

  • Project 2

    This project report talks about what NDVI is and the importance of studying NDVI and preserving plant and land cover. A review of available literature provides insight into the resulting changes and conditions on the territory of Greece. This study aimed to evaluate the potential use of normalized difference vegetation index (NDVI) from satellite-derived remote sensing data. NDVI was determined across habitats with different vegetation types in the landscape.
